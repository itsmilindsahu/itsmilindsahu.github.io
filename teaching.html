03 Theory & Mathematical Patterns

A living document of problem-solving patterns, mathematical insights, and structural observations collected during coursework and self-study.

Vector Calculus
Conservative Vector Fields

A vector field F is conservative if and only if its curl vanishes everywhere. This guarantees path-independence of line integrals and the existence of a potential function φ.

$$ \nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0} \iff \mathbf{F} = \nabla\varphi $$
Linear Algebra
Triangular Matrix Eigenvalues

For any upper or lower triangular matrix, the eigenvalues are exactly the diagonal entries. The characteristic polynomial factors immediately, making computation trivial.

$$ \det(A - \lambda I) = \prod_{i=1}^{n}(a_{ii} - \lambda) $$
Real Analysis
Squeeze Theorem Pattern

When a limit is hard to compute directly, bound the sequence above and below by two sequences converging to the same limit. The target sequence is squeezed to the same value.

$$ g(x) \leq f(x) \leq h(x),\; \lim g = \lim h = L \implies \lim f = L $$
Differential Equations
Separation of Variables

If a first-order ODE can be written so all y-terms are on one side and all x-terms on the other, integrate both sides independently to obtain the general solution.

$$ \frac{dy}{dx} = f(x)g(y) \;\Rightarrow\; \int \frac{dy}{g(y)} = \int f(x)\,dx $$
Multivariable Calculus
Divergence Theorem

The total flux of a vector field through a closed surface equals the volume integral of its divergence. Converts hard surface integrals into often-simpler volume integrals.

$$ \oiint_S \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{S} = \iiint_V (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F})\, dV $$
Linear Algebra
Rank–Nullity Theorem

For any linear map T: V → W, the dimension of the domain equals the rank plus the nullity. A fundamental structural constraint on all linear transformations.

$$ \dim V = \operatorname{rank}(T) + \operatorname{nullity}(T) $$

05 Research & Academic Interests

My academic interests lie at the intersection of pure mathematical structures and their applications to complex real-world systems — particularly in biology, energy, and policy.

I am drawn to problems where rigorous mathematical frameworks can replace heuristic reasoning: modeling biological dynamics with differential equations, applying optimization theory to resource allocation, and using linear-algebraic methods to extract structure from high-dimensional data.

A parallel research interest is mathematical education — understanding why certain topics remain systematically difficult for students, and developing more effective pedagogical approaches rooted in visual and structural intuition rather than purely symbolic manipulation.

I am actively seeking research internship opportunities and graduate collaborations in applied mathematics, mathematical biology, and scientific computing.
