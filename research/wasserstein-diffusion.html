Abstract

The Core Problem

GEOMETRIC FLAW: Existing DDPM-based video summarization models treat annotator scores as Euclidean vectors and average them arithmetically. But annotation scores are probability distributions over temporal segments — they live on a probability simplex, a curved Riemannian manifold. Arithmetic averaging on the simplex produces geometrically invalid results.

This project proposes replacing three core components of the DDPM framework with geometry-aware alternatives that respect the Wasserstein structure of the annotation space, producing a corrected framework that operates on the true manifold of the data.

Proposed Framework

Three-Module Correction

MODULE 01 · AGGREGATION
Wasserstein Barycenter
Replace arithmetic averaging of annotator scores with an optimal transport consensus — the Fréchet mean in Wasserstein space. The W₂ barycenter minimizes the sum of squared Wasserstein distances to each annotator's distribution, ensuring the aggregate is geometrically valid on the simplex.
MODULE 02 · FORWARD PROCESS
Dirichlet Noise Injection
Replace Gaussian noise injection in the DDPM forward process with Dirichlet perturbations. Gaussian noise immediately pushes samples off the probability simplex. Dirichlet noise, parameterized by concentration α(t) → uniform as t → T, keeps all intermediate states as valid probability distributions throughout diffusion.
MODULE 03 · SUPERVISION
Wasserstein Loss
Replace MSE reconstruction loss with a geometry-aware Wasserstein distance supervision signal. MSE penalizes coordinate-wise deviations and ignores distributional structure. The W₂ loss measures how far the denoised distribution deviates from the target in the correct metric space of the annotation simplex.
